# Welcome to Simple Teleprompter

This is the first slide of the demo script. The teleprompter scrolls smoothly through each sentence at a controlled pace. Word counts inform the dwell time so the highlight stays in step with natural speech.

You can press Space to pause or resume playback at any time. Use the arrow keys to step through sentences. Use Shift plus arrow keys to jump whole slides.

## A subheading inside slide one

Subheadings under a slide are shown inline, but they do not create a new slide. Only top-level headings advance the slide cursor.

# Liquid Glass and the Toolbar

Notice the three pills floating at the bottom of the window. The leftmost pill controls play and pause. The centre pill shows the current slide number out of the total.

The rightmost pill displays words per minute and acts as a shortcut into settings. Click it any time to tune the reading speed.

The pills fade out after a few seconds of mouse inactivity while playing. Move the pointer to bring them back.

# Reading Focus Fade

As playback advances, the centre line stays at full brightness. Sentences near the centre dim slightly. Lines further out fade into a soft grey, helping the eye lock onto the current sentence.

Two sliders in settings let you tune this effect. Lower the peripheral opacity for a stronger spotlight. Lower the fade-per-sentence value for a gentler falloff that reads more like a printed page.

The current sentence also wears a subtle accent-coloured chip behind it, so you never lose track of where you are even while scanning ahead.

# Scrolling Pauses Playback

This slide exists to give the scroll-pause-and-resume behaviour something to actually scroll through. Try this once you reach the middle of this slide.

Two-finger scroll on the trackpad anywhere over the script. Playback should pause within a few milliseconds. The toolbar pills come back into view.

Hold still for two seconds. Playback resumes and the cursor smoothly recenters into the middle of the visible region.

Open the settings sheet to turn that auto-resume behaviour off if you prefer to drive playback yourself with the spacebar.

# Tap a Sentence to Seek

Each sentence in the script is clickable. Click any sentence, even far above or below the current cursor, and the teleprompter jumps to it. The highlight follows.

This works the same way for mouse clicks and for future touch interactions on iPadOS, where arrow keys are not available.

If Keynote sync is on, jumping across slide boundaries by clicking will also fire a show-next or show-previous Apple Event to Keynote so the visible deck stays in step.

# Keyboard Cheat Sheet

Space toggles play and pause. The right arrow advances by one sentence. The left arrow steps back by one sentence.

Shift plus right arrow jumps to the next slide. Shift plus left arrow jumps to the previous slide. Both reset the sentence cursor to the start of the destination slide.

Up arrow restarts the current slide from its first sentence. Escape stops playback entirely, or exits fullscreen if you are presenting.

Command plus equals increases the font size in steps of two points. Command plus minus shrinks it. The letter M flips the script horizontally for an optical teleprompter rig.

Command plus comma opens settings. Command plus O opens the file picker. Command plus W closes the window without quitting; the dock icon and any open-recent action will bring it back.

# Final Slide

The final slide closes the demo. You should now have a feel for the toolbar, the sentence highlight, the auto-scroll, the scroll-pause-and-resume, and the keyboard shortcuts. Open settings to customise the experience further.
